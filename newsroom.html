What We Check

Source Credibility

Are citations peer-reviewed? Do named outlets have editorial standards?

Claim Consistency

Do the article's claims align internally and with established record?

Emotional Language

High-affect language and loaded framing are flagged as credibility risks.

Citation Quality

Vague sourcing ("experts say") is weighted against specific attributions.

Publication Context

Satire sites, known misinformation outlets, and unverified blogs are flagged.

ACADEMIC MODE

Uses stricter criteria, claim extraction, and evidence-based prompts. Best for research and high-stakes verification.

Research Standards

Burden of Proof Academic mode requires corroboration from at least two independent, verifiable sources before a claim is considered credible.
Citing a Flagged Article If an article is flagged, note it as "unverified" in your bibliography and seek a primary source for the underlying claim.
Confidence Scores Results include a confidence percentage. Scores below 75% should be treated as inconclusive for academic purposes.
